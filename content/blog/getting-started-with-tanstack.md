The most useful thing AI has done for my marketing practice isn't generating copy. It's compressing the time between brief and execution.

When a founder says "we need a launch campaign by Thursday," the old workflow involved a day of research, another of draft frameworks, and a third of copy rounds. Now the research phase takes two hours. The draft framework takes thirty minutes. That doesn't mean the thinking is gone — it means I can spend the time I saved on the part that actually matters: refinement, judgment, and ensuring the brand voice stays intact.

Here are the tools I use regularly and what they're actually good for:

**Perplexity** for market and competitive research. The citations make it trustworthy and the summaries are genuinely usable, not just web scraped noise.

**Claude** for long-form copy drafts. It holds brand voice better than most tools when you front-load the brief properly. Feed it three examples of copy that represents the brand well, and it extrapolates accurately.

**Gamma** for pitch decks and campaign presentations. Still need design refinement but the structure comes through quickly.

**ChatGPT** for brainstorming campaign names, hashtag options, and creative territories. Volume outputs are its strength — I use it to generate 40 options and cut to 5.

The shift is real and it's not going away. Brands and marketing teams that integrate these tools thoughtfully will move faster and think sharper — not replace thinking altogether.
